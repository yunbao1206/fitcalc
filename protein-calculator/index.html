How Much Protein Do You Need?

Protein is essential for muscle repair, hormone production, immune function, and overall health. Your ideal protein intake depends on your body weight, how active you are, and what you are trying to achieve.

Activity-Based Multipliers

  • Sedentary — 0.8g per kg (minimum RDA recommendation)
  • Lightly active — 1.0g per kg
  • Moderately active — 1.2g per kg
  • Active — 1.4g per kg
  • Very active — 1.6g per kg

Adjusting for Goals and Diet

Goal Adjustments

  • General health — Base multiplier (x1.0)
  • Muscle building — x1.2 increase for muscle protein synthesis
  • Fat loss — x1.3 increase to preserve lean mass in a deficit
  • Athletic performance — x1.4 increase for recovery and performance

Dietary Adjustments

  • Vegetarian — +10% to compensate for lower bioavailability
  • Vegan — +15% to compensate for incomplete amino acid profiles

Frequently Asked Questions

How much protein do I need per day?

The RDA recommends 0.8g per kg of body weight for sedentary adults. Active individuals need 1.0-1.6g per kg, and those building muscle may benefit from 1.6-2.2g per kg.

Do vegetarians and vegans need more protein?

Plant-based proteins are often less bioavailable than animal proteins. Vegetarians may benefit from 10% more protein and vegans from 15% more to compensate for lower digestibility and incomplete amino acid profiles.

When is the best time to eat protein?

Spread your protein intake across 3-4 meals throughout the day. Aim for 0.25-0.4g per kg of body weight per meal to maximize muscle protein synthesis.
